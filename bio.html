Quittner-Strom, Ezekiel B.

A Brief History

Born on a clouded mid-summer's day into the home and hands
of old-world artisans, it seemed the babe knew from the first
that stoicism would be his only. He didn't cry as air warm summer air
filled his lungs. He came into the world blinking slowly over wide eyes.
His Blacksmith Mother and Baker father gave him the name Ezekiel, secretly
hoping that their actions had bound fate, and that this Ezekiel would raise
new life from skeletons and spectres of their family lives. And so he was an
only child, who didn't learn the true meaning of irony until much later in life.
And alas, to his parents dismay, their son shed the name "Ezekiel", instead
adopting the moniker "Zeke". And though he was literate, and perhaps even intelligent
he found pleasure only in physical labor. A part of himself he still stuggles with
at university, to this very day.
